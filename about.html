About Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as the "Missile Man of India", was one of the most respected scientists, teachers, and leaders in Indian history. He was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a humble family. Despite financial difficulties, he worked hard in his studies and developed a strong interest in science and technology from a young age.

Dr. Kalam studied Physics at St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later completed Aerospace Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT). After graduation, he joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). At ISRO, he played a key role in the successful launch of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which placed the Rohini satellite into orbit in 1980.

He made remarkable contributions to India's defence and missile development programmes. Under his leadership, India successfully developed missiles such as Agni and Prithvi, earning him the title "Missile Man of India." He also played an important role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests conducted in 1998, strengthening India's defence capabilities.

Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. During his presidency, he became known as the "People's President" because of his simplicity, honesty, and close interaction with students and young people. He believed that the youth of India had the power to transform the nation through education, innovation, and hard work.

He received many prestigious honours, including the Padma Bhushan (1981), Padma Vibhushan (1990), and Bharat Ratna (1997), India's highest civilian award. He also received honorary doctorates from universities across the world for his contributions to science, technology, and education.

Dr. Kalam was also a renowned author. His famous books include Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, India 2020, My Journey, and Turning Points. These books continue to inspire millions of readers, especially students and young professionals.

On 27 July 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) Shillong, Dr. Kalam collapsed and passed away. He dedicated his final moments to teaching students, reflecting his lifelong passion for education. His life remains a symbol of dedication, humility, patriotism, and the pursuit of knowledge.
