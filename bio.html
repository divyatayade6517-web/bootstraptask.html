Biography

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He completed his studies in Aerospace Engineering and began his career at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). Later, he joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he played a major role in developing India's satellite launch vehicles and missile programmes.

Because of his outstanding contributions to science and technology, he became known as the "Missile Man of India." He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and was loved by people of all ages, especially students.

Dr. Kalam received many prestigious awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour. His famous books include Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020. He passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong, leaving behind a legacy of knowledge, humility, and inspiration.
